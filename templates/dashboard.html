How the Greedy Algorithm Works

The greedy approach computes each participant's net balance (total received − total paid). Participants with a negative balance owe money (debtors); those with positive balance are owed money (creditors). We repeatedly settle the largest debt against the largest credit until all balances are zero.

1
Compute Net Balances

For each person: net = sum(incoming) − sum(outgoing). This collapses all indirect debts into a single number.

2
Merge Sort Balances

Sort debtors (ascending debt) and creditors (descending credit) using Merge Sort — O(n log n) guaranteed.

3
Greedy Selection

Pick max debtor and max creditor. Settle settle = min(|debt|, credit). This is the greedy "locally optimal" choice.

4
Update & Repeat

Subtract settled amount from both. Advance pointer for whichever reached zero. Repeat until done.

5
Output Optimized List

The result is globally optimal — the minimum number of transactions needed to settle all debts.
